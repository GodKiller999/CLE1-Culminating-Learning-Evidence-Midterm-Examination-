Race/Color Discrimination

Racial discrimination occurs when an individual (a candidate or employee) is treated unfairly due to their race or due to racialized personal traits (such skin tone, hair type, or specific facial features). Treating someone unfairly according to their skin tone is known as color discrimination. Treating someone unfairly just because they are married to, or connected to, someone of a particular race or color is another example of race or color discrimination. When the discriminating party and the victim share the same race or color, discrimination may take place.
